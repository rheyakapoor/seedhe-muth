LUNCH BREAK

Lunchbreak serves as a vibrant interlude between Seedhe Maut's previous album, "Nayaab," and their upcoming release, "Kavi Kehna Chahte Hain." This unique tape not only marks a distinctive pause but also showcases a departure from their usual style by featuring collaborations with several artists, a departure from their typical approach.

NAYAAB

Four years on from the release of an album that redefined Indian hip-hop, Seedhe Maut and Sez On The Beat reunite for an explosive sophomore album that expands upon the story of three friends attempting to break the mold and create a path for future generations to follow. Building on the juxtaposition of grandeur and grime that permeates every facet of life in the nation’s capital, Nayaab is a vivid portrayal of a city that loves, hates and makes you told by three of the finest creative minds this country has ever seen.

न

Conceptualised and recorded in Goa, न is a celebration of the journey that the duo and the #SeedheMautNation have taken together over the past couple of years. With two albums – made in collaboration with Sez On The Beat and Ritviz – in the pipeline for 2021, न offers a more relaxed, casual insight opportunity for fans to experience the creative and personal relationship that Calm and Encore enjoy as Seedhe Maut.

BAYAAN

With fiery flows and politically charged verses, Seedhe Maut have cemented their place as the bilingual poster boys of Azadi Boys. On their debut album, Encore ABJ (right) and Calm (left) channel urban angst and tackle the sociopolitical issues of modern Indian youth. Album highlight “Shaktimaan” features duo rhyming themes of self-worth and celebrating the ‘90s TV superhero and they add furious lyrics to Sez On The Beat’s (middle) haunting flute sample and blistering sub-bass.
